About

An analytical mind trained in science, now applied to business.

Every model I build still follows the same instinct pharmacy taught me first: test the hypothesis, trust the data, then act.

I began my academic life in a laboratory, not a boardroom — earning my Bachelor of Pharmacy from the Noida Institute of Engineering & Technology with First Division with Distinction, under a fully government-funded scholarship. It was a discipline built on evidence: a hypothesis means nothing until it survives scrutiny. That habit of mind — question first, decide second — is what carried me into business.

Across internships spanning pharmaceutical manufacturing, hospital operations, and most recently corporate finance at Hindalco (Aditya Birla Group), I found the common thread I was looking for: every function, whatever the industry, runs on the same underlying logic of process, compliance, and financial trade-off. That realization is what pulled me toward an MBA in Financial Consultancy & Operations at the Mittal School of Business, LPU, where I now build project finance and LBO models, dissect unit economics, and study how capital decisions actually get made.

I'm drawn to the parts of finance that resemble diagnosis more than arithmetic — reading a balance sheet the way I once read a lab result, looking for what the numbers are quietly telling you. That instinct now shows up in my project finance models, my research on financial literacy, and my growing interest in strategy and operations consulting, where analytical precision has to translate into a decision I'm willing to bet on.

Business Problem

Evaluate whether a 1,000-unit EV charging network deployed across six Indian metro cities could be financed and operated as a viable independent project.

Approach

Built a 10-year project finance model incorporating capex phasing, debt structuring, and operating-cost assumptions across each metro market.

Methodology

Cash flow forecasting, debt capacity analysis, and return-on-investment assessment under a defined capital structure and financing scenario.

Key Contributions

  • Built the full capex and financing schedule from scratch
  • Modeled debt sizing against projected cash flow coverage

Business Impact & Results

The model demonstrated strong investment viability — a 38.0% Equity IRR and 14.4% Project IRR, with positive project and equity NPVs of ₹14,100L and ₹3,665L respectively — supporting the case for the network's bankability.

Learning Outcome

Deepened fluency in structuring infrastructure-style financing — balancing debt capacity against long-horizon operating risk in an emerging asset class.

Business Problem

Determine a defensible selling price and target cost for a proposed consumer product, "Fold & Go," to sustain a healthy margin against competitors.

Approach

Ran competitor benchmarking and demand-based pricing analysis to anchor the price point, then reverse-engineered a target cost structure.

Methodology

Built a cost model covering direct costs, overheads, and distribution expense to compare actual versus target cost.

Key Contributions

  • Identified an unfavourable ₹6.77 per-unit cost variance
  • Recommended sourcing and logistics levers to close the gap

Business Impact & Results

Set a ₹300 selling price against a ₹250 target cost, with sourcing and logistics recommendations designed to sustain a 16.7% target margin despite the identified variance.

Learning Outcome

Reinforced how pricing decisions must be reconciled against cost structure early — not after a product is already committed to production.
